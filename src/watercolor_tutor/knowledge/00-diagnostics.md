# Diagnostics & Technique Glossary

Shared reference across all steps. Named techniques and a fault → cause → fix rubric.

## Technique glossary
- **Flat wash**: a single even layer of one color, uniform from edge to edge.
- **Graded wash**: a wash that fades smoothly from dark to light by adding water to the
  mix as you move down (or pigment, to go light-to-dark).
- **Wet-on-wet**: applying paint onto already-wet paper, so colors spread and blend with
  soft, feathery edges. Unpredictable and atmospheric — good for skies.
- **Wet-on-dry**: applying paint onto dry paper, giving crisp, controlled, hard edges.
- **Glazing**: laying a transparent wash over a fully *dry* previous layer to deepen or
  shift color without disturbing it. Each layer must dry completely first.
- **Lifting**: removing pigment from a still-damp (or re-wetted) wash with a thirsty
  brush or tissue to create highlights or corrections.

## Fault → cause → fix
- **Backrun / cauliflower / bloom**: a feathery blotch where water pushed pigment outward.
  Cause: dropping water or wet paint into a half-dry area. Fix: let washes dry undisturbed;
  lift the final bead at the bottom of a wash.
- **Streaking / banding**: visible stripes in a wash. Cause: brush underloaded, mix too
  dry, ran out of mixture mid-wash, or worked too slowly. Fix: mix extra, keep the brush
  loaded, work at a steady pace, maintain a wet edge.
- **Muddy color**: dull, grey-brown mixes. Cause: mixing more than 3 pigments together, or
  mixing with dirty rinse water. Fix: limit mixes to 2–3 pigments; mix with clean water.
- **Unwanted hard edges**: a sharp line where a wash dried with a defined boundary. Cause:
  a wash edge left to dry without being carried along. Fix: keep a wet edge, or soften with
  a thirsty brush before it dries.
- **Buckling / warping paper**: Cause: paper too lightweight for the water load. Fix: use
  140 lb+ paper and tape it down on all edges.

## Core principles
- **Watercolor dries lighter** than it looks when wet — by roughly 20–30%. Mix a touch
  stronger than the target.
- **Work light to dark**: you can darken later, but you can't easily lighten (white comes
  from the paper, not paint).
- **Keep a wet edge**: most wash problems trace back to letting an edge dry before the next
  stroke meets it.
